FAQ

What is hackGSU?

hackGSU is the Hackathon hosted by the Georgia State University Computer Science Department's ACM & IEEE student organizations. It is 100% free of charge, lots of food, free swag, & $$ prizes $$ . 500+ students from all across the country attended our first hackGSU, last Spring, and we are expecting 400+ participants this Fall.

When and where is it?

hackGSU Fall '16 will take place from October 21st to 23rd in the heart of Atlanta at the beautiful Georgia State University campus.

Who can participate?

All university students*! You do not have to be a computer science major to participate in the competition! If you're someone who is willing to learn and wants to take a shot at changing the world, no matter what your field is, you definitely belong here.

How much does it cost?

Nothing! Zero! The event is absolutely free for all participants. We will provide you with the venue, food, Wi-Fi, etc. You just need to show up.

I would like to come from outside of Georgia, is there travel reimbursement?

Yes, please come, we welcome students from all across the country to attend hackGSU. We will provide up to $200 in individual travel reimbursement this year, while funds are available. Please let us know in advance, via email, if you live out of state so that we can coordinate with you! (If we are not notified in advance of your travel reimbursement request, unfortunately we cannot guarantee that we will be able to reimburse you.)

I have never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?

We are glad that hackGSU will be your first hackathon. There will be mentors to help you and we will be running workshops for you to learn skills from. We all start somewhere, why not here?

But I don't have an idea, what should I do?

Not a problem! We will walk you through how to generate great ideas at the beginning of hackGSU.

How about teams?

Teams have a maximum of 4. If you don't have a team, don't worry. Once the idea generation is done, there will be an open mic and networking time to find a team. In addition, after you have registered, you also will be invited to the hackGSU Facebook group prior to the hackathon in order to discuss ideas and teams.

What should I bring?

You will definitely need your laptop, phone, their chargers, as well as a valid student or government ID. Feel free to also bring along a sleeping bag, and we recommend some comfortable clothes (and a hoodie!).

When is the deadline to register?

The deadline to register is on October 20th at 11:59pm EST.

How can I confirm that my registration was received for HackGSU?

Login to your my.mlh.io account, and choose My Profile > Authorized Apps. Your HackGSU registration will display, including the date that you registered.

How do I obtain travel reimbursement?

We will provide up to $200 in individual travel reimbursement this year, while funds are available (see above). To obtain the travel reimbursement, you must have receipts. You must not live or go to school in Georgia to be eligible for travel reimbursement. Funds are dispersed on a first-come, first-served basis, based on the timestamp of your pre-event email request. Examples of eligible travel expenses include gas, airline tickets, etc. There will be plenty of free food at the HackGSU event, so we are not reimbursing food expenses. We are not reimbursing hotel expenses at this time (you are welcome to bring a sleeping bag).

Is there a Code of Conduct?

Yes, we abide by the Major League Hacking Code of Conduct.

*I'm in highschool and I would like to attend...

If you're local to Georgia, please register!If you're not from Georgia, please register, and make sure to drop us a note so that we can help reimburse travel expenses (while funds last).

I have another question...

Please feel free to email us at hackathon@cs.gsu.edu for any questions you have!
